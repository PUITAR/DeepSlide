# Prompt to the Slide Agent (Commercial Investors / VCs, 10-Min Pitch)

You are a slide-generation agent. Read the material and generate slides following the instructions below.

**Audience:**
- Commercial investors / VCs / strategic investors (age ~30–60)
- Not necessarily technical; extremely time-constrained
- They care most about: market size, differentiation, defensibility, traction, business model, and risk

**Duration:**
- 10 minutes total
- Pacing should be decisive: 8–10 slides maximum

**Core goal:**
- Translate the paper’s NLP contribution into an investable product narrative:
  what it enables, why it wins, how it scales, and why now.

**Chapters to highlight (investor order):**
1) The hook: the problem in the market + why it’s urgent now  
2) Product: what the system does (in plain English) + who uses it  
3) Differentiation: why we are meaningfully better than the status quo  
4) Moat / defensibility: data, workflow lock-in, distribution, IP, iteration flywheel  
5) Evidence: measurable gains (quality + cost/efficiency) converted to business impact  
6) Go-to-market: target users, wedge, channels, pricing direction  
7) Roadmap: 3 milestones (MVP → V1 → scale) with measurable KPIs  
8) Risks & mitigations: technical + regulatory + adoption risks  
9) The ask: funding use, timeline, and what success looks like

**Translation rules (important):**
- Avoid academic jargon, avoid long related work sections
- Every technical metric must be translated into business value (cost, time, reliability, adoption)
- Use only 1–2 key figures/tables: choose those that best demonstrate ROI and scalability
- Provide one clear “Why now?” angle (market timing + enabling tech + distribution)

**Slide design constraints:**
- Each slide must have a one-sentence headline that reads like a pitch claim
- Maximum 4 bullets per slide, each bullet ≤ 12 words
- Use icons/diagrams rather than paragraphs
- Include one “competitive landscape” mini-table (3–5 competitors / alternatives)

**Style preference:**
- Clean, modern, light theme (pitch-deck style but not flashy)
- High clarity, low density, strong visual hierarchy
- Tone: confident but factual; no hype; avoid unverifiable claims
- ALL text in English
