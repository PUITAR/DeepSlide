Let’s start by understanding the two dominant approaches in LLM post-training. First, Supervised Fine-Tuning, or SFT, where models learn from carefully curated datasets of input-output pairs. This method is great for aligning model behavior with human demonstrations, as seen in systems like FLAN or LLaMA. However, SFT has limitations—it’s static and struggles with dynamic environments. On the other hand, Reinforcement Learning, or RL, optimizes models by interacting with environments and receiving feedback signals like rewards. While powerful, RL requires extensive data collection and environment interaction, which can be inefficient. Together, these approaches have strengths but also clear gaps when used in isolation.
